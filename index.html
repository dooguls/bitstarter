Hack the Man is meant to be a somewhat technically accurate, yet tounge in cheek, card game which allows you, the players, to take down your favorite shadowy government Agency. Given all the press lately, it seemed like going after NSA as the inaugural punching bag would be most entertaining. Though we fully plan to provide expansion packs to go after CIA and FBI in the States, BND in Germany, GCHQ in the UK (how come they can't just use 3 letters anyway?), FSB in Russia, and MSS in China. Of course the further expansion packs can be reordered based on contributions and popularity...

The basic premise is you have an objective to accomplish, which can be short or long based on how much time you have to play. The objective is accomplished by hacking into the agency's computers and stealing some secret, planting false information, or taking down the whole thing. You can play with between 2 and 8 people. Players can play 'co-op' against the agency, or against each other. In fact you can even scoop your teammates and come out on top. Who doesn't like a little 'screw-your-neighbor' anyway? There are cool hacker tricks and surprises to keep you from your objective.

FAQ

How many people can play?
As few as 2 and as many as 8.
What does the game cost?
The base game is $15. Expansions are $8.
How long does the game take to play?
Depends on the number of people, and objective selected. A short two player game can be 20min. A long 8 player game can run 60minutes.
